Instawork

Jan 2018 - Oct 2018

Instawork is a design concept for an Android app created for a work related project that spanned over 9 months.

See demo video under "View Demo" at the bottom.

Role: Lead UI/UX designer, UX Researcher

Tools: Adobe XD, Adobe Photoshop CC, Lucidchart

Background: This project consisted of trying to understand different workplace enviornments and how to improve workflow and collaboration throughout offices.

Research: To identify the problem, validate our solution and understand our users, a number of research methods were utilized. This included reading papers and articles to cover preliminary research of problems, enviornments, and potential users.

  • Field Studies: This was conducted to gauge open and closed workspace floorplans and how work enviornment may affect work habits. This involved visiting shared workspace environments (e.g. companies and coworking spaces) to observe workers in their natural environment and gain insight into user’s behavioral patterns.
  • Phone Surveys: I formulated survey questions, analyzed and drew conclusions from target customers. This method was used to validate whether our product was going in the right direction and understand customer's backgrounds and frustrations.
  • Roleplay: Roleplaying was used as an initial method to test out the project’s design concept on potential users and verify how the scenarios worked. Mockups were created for screens and phones, I acted as the system and a user depending on the scenario and user testers were given a role to act out. Interviews were conducted at the end and findings were pieced together using design thinking methodlogies.

    SHARP Smart Office Assistant

    Fall 2016 - Winter 2018

    A proof of concept (PoC) Android application designed to facilitate and enhance the productivity of meetings developed for our sponsors (Sharp Labs) over 6 months in a group of 5.

    See demo under "View Demo" at the bottom.

    Role:
    • Lead frontend designer that developed 95% of UI & connected frontend with the backend
    • UI & UX Designer including creating wireframes, scenarios, personas and user testing
    • Worked on all documentations

    Design Tools: Balsamiq, Lucidchart, Adobe Photoshop CC

    Coding/Tools: Java, Android Studio, Eclipse, Node.JS, Postman

    Problem: Meetings are often unproductive and inefficient due to a number of factors including a lack of agenda, being poorly planned and organized, being off topic or over the allotted time frame and a lack of member participation.

    Solution: Smart Office Assistant is a PoC Android app designed to facilitate and enhance the productivity of meetings for organizations and companies. Smart Office Assistant aims to tackle the problem by offering a mobile solution to keep the management of meetings and all related meeting activities all in one place.

    Functionalities offered in PoC:

    • Displaying all of the user’s meeting information on one screen
    • Creating, deleting, and updating meetings
    • Creating, deleting, and updating meeting topics for each meeting
    • Adding and saving notes for each meeting topic
    • Adding a timer and starting a timer for each topic
    • Interactive Chatbot to create, update or delete meetings automatically (synced with a user’s Google Calendar)
    Deliverables:
    • A functional PoC Android application (with responsive design in mind for phone & tablet)
    • Project Plan, User Requirements, Business Case, Software & UI Design document, Test Cases, Test Plans, Usability Tests, Results, & Evaluations, suggested changes and improvements document
    Use Case Diagram

    Storyboard Examples

    Reflection: For this project, I learned more on how to build in native Android, making API calls, working with a real client, and putting my design skills to use. Because this was a PoC, there were many changes both in UI design and with features that we would have liked to have changed or included, but given the time frame, was not possible. For the design, I chose different shades of red because that went along with Sharp's company colors. I maintained a consistent use of dark reds for text fields, a brighter red for buttons with white text for contrast, and gray for headings and informative text. If time permitted, I would have liked to have cleaned up the UI design and overall UX because some designs on the Smart Office Assitant app were implemented simply for it to be functional instead of to provide a good UX. For example, the display of meetings on the Home screen is not very asesthetically pleasing and could be displayed in a much less cluttered format.

    Pettr

    A prototype web application built for users to match with potential pets for adoption. This was created in a group of 4 for my Project in User Interaction Software Class in the span of 10 weeks. We also conducted user tests and results.

    See demo video under "View Demo" at the bottom.

    Role: UI/UX Designer, Front-End Web Developer, User Tester

    Tools/Coding: Balsamiq, Adobe Photoshop CC, HTML/CSS, React.JS

    Functional Features:
    • Sign up & Login
    • Create, Update & View User Profile
    • Create, Update & View Pet Profiles
    • Browse All Available Pets
    • Choose to "Adopt" or "Don't Adopt" Pets
    • View Mutual Matches & Connect

    Problem: Pet shelters are often overburdened with the amount of pets they take in and when pets are not adopted within a certain time frame, pets are often put down. Owners looking for new pets often resort to buying pets as opposed to adopting those that need a home.

    Solution: In an effort to save more pets, we created Pettr to give a platform for pet owners, potential pet owners and pet shelters to connect. This gives those looking to adopt pets an option to find their dream pet on our platform. Future ideas for expansion and improvements includ allowing pet play dates or temporary pet sitting to be set up.

    Process
    • Reaching Out: We contacted local pet shelters to explain our idea and ask if we could use their their database of real pets in the shelter to put onto our web application.
    • Competitive Analysis: We did research on competitor products to see which kind of pet adoption apps (mobile or web) already existed and how we would differentiate from them.
    • Paper Prototypes: Each of our team members sketched out basic ideas of how we envisioned the web app would look like, before we compared ideas and brainstormed for the best design and user experience.
    • Wireframes: Balsamiq was used to put together a mobile and web wireframes based off the agreed upon idea from our paper sketches.
    • Building Web App: My team was divided up so everyone worked with their strengths. I was in charge of UI/UX design and front end development.
    • User Testing: Following the completion of a functional prototype, we began user testing.
      • Tasks: Target users were given a series of tasks to complete on our web app, while I observed and took notes. Tasks included creating an account, setting up their profile, adding and viewing pet profiles, browsing through all pets, experiencing "adopt" vs "don't adopt" functions and what happens when there is a confirmed match.
      • Interviews: Following completion of tasks, follow up questions were asked of users to receieve feedback on the effectiveness and difficulty of the web app.

    Personal Website From Scratch

    A fully responsive basic personal website created from scratch using HTML, CSS, and Javascript for my User Interaction Software class. This website was created without Bootstrap or any templates with "mobile first" design in mind. For this project, user research and user testing was also completed with a full analysis of changes that I would have liked to have implemented.

    See full report under "View Site" at the bottom.

    I used the following methods on this project:

    • User Interviews - to better understand the possible users and their different expectations from the product.
    • Competitor Analysis - to evaluate the features, strengths and weaknesses of my website in comparison to others' website in my field.
    • Feature Value Matrix & Feature Prioritization - to rank the features that were implemented and what features should be implemented in order of value based off complexity and importance.
    • Cognitive Walkthrough - to evaluate the effectiveness and success of the website by giving a set of tasks to user testers to complete/answer.
    • User Stories - to explain the reasoning behind existing features and features that I wanted to implement that will provide a better user interface and experience.
    • User Test - Used a click test and a 5 second test to gauge what the first impression of the website is.
